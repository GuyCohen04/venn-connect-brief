01 / The Problem

Reps aren't losing on the pitch. They're losing before the call starts.

Venn's ICP spans tourism operators, construction firms, digital agencies, import/export businesses, and any Canadian SMB with high card spend and a rough relationship with the Big 5. Typically 15 to 100 employees. Once your reps connect with the right decision-maker, the product sells itself: 0.25% FX spread vs. 2–3% bank spreads[1], 2% interest on CAD and USD balances, zero monthly fees. The value is real and the switching cost is low.

The breakdown happens one step earlier. Apollo's data doesn't verify for these buyers. ZoomInfo is too expensive to scale across the team. These owners aren't on LinkedIn, and the controllers who are don't answer InMail. Reps burn hours dialing numbers that go nowhere, hitting receptionists trained to block, or landing voicemails that never get returned.

The real problem isn't that Venn has bad data. It's that "connect rate" is a compound metric, and every layer of it is broken for this ICP.
Connect Rate, Decomposed
Right Number × Answer Rate × Right Person = Connect
If Apollo gives you a 15% accurate number × a 20% answer rate (industry B2B cold benchmark)[2] × a 50% right-person rate (controller gatekept behind reception), the math lands at ~1.5% connect per dial. Even doubling number accuracy only gets you to 3%. Fixing one layer doesn't fix the rate. Each layer has to be attacked independently.

This matters because rep output is capped by whichever layer is worst. Across the team, even a 2-percentage-point lift in connect rate compounds into dozens of additional qualified conversations per week. And conversations are what Venn closes on.

02 / Not All of Venn's ICP Is Equally Hard

Within the ICP, the connect problem varies sharply by segment.

Venn's ICP isn't monolithic. Some segments (digital agencies, food & beverage operators under 35 owners) are reachable through standard outbound. Others (construction, import/export, hotel operators) require a fundamentally different motion. Any plan that treats them the same will miss.

03 / The Framework

Four parallel channels. Each attacks a different layer of the connect rate equation.

No single channel fixes this. Adding one new data source gets you marginal gains. The right play is to run four channels in parallel, measure each one's contribution to connect rate independently, and let the data tell us where to double down.

01  ·  The verification waterfall
Fixes: Right Number
Rather than replacing Apollo, build a multi-stage verification layer on top of it. Every contact passes through a waterfall that enriches with free and low-cost Canadian-native data sources. Apollo becomes the top of funnel. The waterfall is what turns raw exports into reps-can-actually-dial-this lists.
How the waterfall works
1
Apollo export (starting list)
Pull target accounts by industry, size, and geography. Accept that the numbers and emails are roughly 15% accurate out of the box. This is the starting point, not the finished product.
2
Provincial corporate registry match
Match each account against Ontario Business Registry, BC Registries, Alberta Corporate Registry, and Quebec Registraire des entreprises[8]. Director and officer names are public record for every incorporated business in Canada. This surfaces the actual owner's legal name, often missing from Apollo for single-owner SMBs.
3
Google Business + website enrichment
For each account, pull the verified Google Business phone (the line owners actually monitor for customer calls, not the reception switchboard), scrape the company website for About and Contact pages, and extract owner emails commonly listed in footers or leadership sections of small-business sites.
4
Scott's Directories cross-reference
Cross-check against Scott's Directories[9], a Canada-only B2B database with 945,000+ verified company profiles and 2.5M contacts, including director-level data Apollo misses. For a fraction of ZoomInfo's cost, this fills most of the remaining gaps for Tier C accounts.
5
Manual spot-check on top 100 accounts
For the highest-value targets, manually verify against LinkedIn searches, OpenCorporates filings, and domain WHOIS records. Time-intensive but worth it for the 20% of accounts driving 80% of pipeline value.
6
Salesforce-ready output
Every verified contact gets tagged with confidence scoring, source attribution, and segment classification. Reps see which numbers are highest-confidence before they dial. A single CSV becomes a tiered call list, not a pile of guesses.
Why this works better than buying ZoomInfo: the Canadian corporate registry data is free, structured, and legally public. Apollo and ZoomInfo don't integrate it natively because they're built globally. A purpose-built Canadian waterfall catches every incorporated SMB in Venn's ICP. It also gives Venn a defensible moat: your competitors can buy the same Apollo list, they can't easily replicate this pipeline.
02  ·  Channel diversification beyond phone
Fixes: Answer Rate
Test CASL-compliant SMS, direct mail for high-value accounts in dense metros (Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary), and in-person drop-ins for the top 50 target accounts in Tier C. B2B SMS has measurably higher open rates than email, and physical mail cuts through an empty channel because almost zero fintechs are doing it to this segment.
Why it works: Tier C owners don't answer cold calls from 1-800 numbers, but they read texts and they notice physical mail on their desk. Venn's direct competitors (Float, Loop, Relay)[1] won't run this motion because it's slower, harder to measure, and requires operational discipline. That's exactly why it's an edge.
03  ·  Accountant referral motion
Fixes: Right Person + Trust
Identify the 200 to 300 Canadian accounting firms serving construction, tourism, digital agencies, and import/export SMBs. Build a partner motion: Venn's QuickBooks and Xero integrations[1] give accountants a real product reason to recommend it. One accountant introduction converts orders of magnitude better than a cold call because it arrives with trust pre-installed.
Why it works: Tier C owners don't trust fintechs, but they trust their accountants implicitly. For most SMBs in this segment, the accountant is the de facto CFO. A referral motion here sidesteps the data problem entirely. The accountant hands you the controller's direct line and says "talk to them."
04  ·  Chamber + BIA partnership play
Fixes: Volume at the top
Target the 30 highest-density Chambers of Commerce and Business Improvement Areas (BIAs) in Canada. Toronto alone has approximately 80 BIAs[10]. Every metro has a chamber. Their business model is delivering member value. A co-branded email from the Mississauga Chamber to its construction-heavy member list carries orders-of-magnitude more weight than a Venn cold email, and the chamber is motivated to send it because it delivers real savings (FX and interest) to their members.
Why this ships in 8 weeks: unlike national industry associations (slow, board-gated, relationship-heavy), local chambers and BIAs move fast and have small teams. The deal is simple: Venn offers members a preferred rate or onboarding incentive, the chamber sends one co-branded email per month. Within 2 months, this produces measurable meeting volume from warm lists. It also fixes Tier C because BIAs index heavily on construction, hospitality, and import/export, exactly Venn's hardest segments to reach cold.
04 / 8-Week Execution Plan

Build, test, measure, scale.

The goal isn't to run all four channels at full volume from day one. It's to build each one in sequence, establish a baseline, run measured experiments, and by week 8 hand Venn a playbook with clear data on what's working.

Weeks 1–2Baseline + waterfall build

Understand current state. Build the verification waterfall.

  • Sit with 2 to 3 reps on outbound calls. Log current connect rate per channel, segmented by Tier A / B / C.
  • Pull baseline metrics from Salesforce: dials, connects, meetings booked, conversion per segment.
  • Build stages 1 to 4 of the verification waterfall: Apollo export, provincial registry match, Google Business enrichment, Scott's cross-reference.
  • Produce a verified contact list of 500 Tier C accounts as Channel 1 proof-of-concept. Measure number accuracy lift over raw Apollo.
Weeks 3–4Channel 2 + waterfall refinement

Launch SMS, direct mail, walk-in tests. Refine waterfall stages 5 to 6.

  • Draft CASL-compliant SMS templates, test on 200 Tier C accounts. Measure open, reply, meeting-booked rates.
  • Send physical mail to the top 50 accounts, handwritten card with QR code to a Venn landing page. Track unique opens.
  • Run a 1-week Toronto metro walk-in experiment for the top 20 accounts. Measure meeting conversion.
  • Complete waterfall stages 5 (manual spot-check) and 6 (Salesforce-ready output with confidence scoring).
Weeks 5–6Channel 3 launch

Build the accountant referral motion.

  • Identify 200 accounting firms serving construction, tourism, digital agencies, and import/export SMBs. Segment by city and book size.
  • Draft the accountant partner pitch: Venn's QuickBooks/Xero integration, referral incentive, co-marketing angle.
  • Run outreach to 50 firms. Target 5 to 10 meetings. Track which firms yield actual client referrals within the engagement.
  • Build referral tracking inside Salesforce so Venn can continue the motion after week 8.
Weeks 7–8Channel 4 + handoff

Chamber/BIA partnerships. Packaged playbook. Measured results.

  • Contact 30 priority chambers and BIAs (Toronto, Mississauga, Vancouver, Calgary, Montreal, Ottawa). Propose co-branded member offers.
  • Close 3 to 5 partnership pilots with signed terms. Execute at least 2 member-list campaigns before week 8.
  • Consolidate all four channels into a single dashboard: cost per connect, meeting rate, pipeline contribution.
  • Run a handoff session with Alexandra and the rep team. Document which channels to scale, which to sunset.
05 / How We Measure Success

Three numbers that tell the whole story.

Everything I build is instrumented. Venn doesn't hire me for 2 months and wonder if it worked. Every experiment has a baseline, a target, and a measurable output.

Primary metric
Connect rate lift
Measured per rep, per channel, per ICP tier (A/B/C). Target: meaningful, attributable lift against week-1 baseline. Exact target set once baseline is established.
Secondary metric
Meetings booked
The number that actually matters to the team's quota. Baseline weekly meeting rate per rep, segmented by channel source, tracked for 8 weeks.
Leading indicator
Cost per connect
Compares channel efficiency: SMS vs. direct mail vs. accountant referral vs. chamber partnership vs. baseline phone. Tells Venn where to allocate rep time after the engagement.

These metrics sit in a dashboard Alexandra can check weekly. No surprises, no end-of-engagement reveal. If a channel isn't working by week 4, we kill it and reallocate.
